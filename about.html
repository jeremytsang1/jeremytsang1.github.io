Reading List

One of my favorite hobbies is reading (and listening to audiobooks). Lately I've been reading a lot of fantasy and science fiction novels. In no particular order, here are some that I've read in the past few years.

title author format genre thoughts
Hyperion Cantos Dan Simmons series sci-fi Novels that will make one rethink faith, love, time, and reality.
First Law Joe Abercrombie series fantasy Some of the best character development I've ever encountered.
Abhorsen Garth Nix series fantasy A necromancer going off to fight evil using magical bells, what's not to like?
Stormlight Archive Brandon Sanderson series fantasy Absolutely amazing world building.
The Stand Stephen King standalone sci-fi/fantasy Entertaining characters and high stakes story, but cop-out ending.
The Devil All the Time Donald Ray Pollock standalone thriller Gritty, dark, violent. Couldn't put it down.
Replay Ken Grimwood standalone sci-fi/romance Interesting little story about time travel.
Discworld Terry Pratchett series fantasy Only read 6 of the 41 but Night Watch was profound compared to the other 5.

Powerlifting

Physical fitness is always an important maintain not only for personal health but as an outlet to relieve stress when other parts of life get stressful. In particular training for competitions, are also a great way to build discipline and consistently work to an achieve a concrete goal. Although I don't compete as much now that I am in school I would love to once I finish school (and the COVID-19 situation ends).

This video is from an old meet back in 2016. It was my first sanctioned USAPL meet that I competed in. I was so wrapped up in the lifts that on my first attempt I forgot to wait for the re-rack command and ended up getting red-lighted! However my second and third attempts went just fine to the extend that I even managed to make a personal record that day. Video owned by Jeremy Tsang.
